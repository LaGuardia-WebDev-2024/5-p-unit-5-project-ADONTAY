Visit Grenada The Spice Isle

Grenada, is a country with lots of importance in history and culture in Europe from when Columbus sighted it.Christopher Columbus named the island "La Concepcion" in 1498. Spanish sailors may have renamed the island "La Granada" after the city of Granada. . Grenada is called the "Spice Isle" because of its production of nutmeg, mace, cinnamon, and cloves.

Fun Facts

  • Grenada is a volcanic island formed by volcanic activity about 2 million years ago
  • The island is also known as 'The Island of Spice' due to it's abundance of spice plantations.
  • There’s a lake in the heart of an extinct volcano that you can visit
  • The National flower of Grenada is the Bougainveilla
  • The National Bird of Grenada is the Leptotila Wellsi
  • Grenada’s rich history can be seen today through it’s storied forts.
  • Grenada is home to a mesmerising underwater sculpture park
